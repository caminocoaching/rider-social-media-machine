### Example: Practice vs. Race Gap

---

We've talked about the practice-to-race gap and what it's costing you.

Now let's talk about *why* it happens.

In 2006, a Stanford psychologist named Carol Dweck identified two fundamental beliefs people hold about their abilities.

**Fixed mindset:** Your talent is largely innate. You either have it or you don't.

**Growth mindset:** Abilities can be developed through effort, learning, and persistence.

Here's what neuroscientists discovered:

These beliefs create *measurably different patterns of brain activity*.

When you make a mistake on track, your brain generates two signals.

The first detects the error. Everyone's brain does this.

The second determines how much attention you allocate to *learning* from that error.

Growth mindset drivers show significantly larger learning signals.
Fixed mindset drivers dismiss mistakes quickly and move on.

**Same mistake. Same corner. Same car. Completely different brain responses.**

Here's what this means for you:

In testing, you're in growth mode. Experimenting. Curious. Learning.

In qualifying, your brain switches to protection mode. It treats the result as a threat to your identity as a driver.

And when that happens, it literally shuts down access to your fluid, skilled performance.

The good news?

This neural pattern is trainable. Forty years of research proves it.
